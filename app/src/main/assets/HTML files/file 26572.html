The easiest method is to get a proper certificate, with a correct DN and signed by a public CA, on each machine on which you are deploying the service. It's bureaucratic and annoying and probably costs real money, but it is definitely easiest overall.

Otherwise, you have to configure the clients to have a verifier that doesn't actually verify. That's dangerous, since anyone at all (including random hackers, organised criminals and dodgy government agencies) can make a self-signed certificate and there's no practical way to detect that they have done so.
