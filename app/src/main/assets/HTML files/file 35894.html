Wordnet maps and relates senses not words. So looking for related synonyms might not be possible in WordNet. Also you fall into the problem of words with multiple senses; without the context, the senses of any word is unclear:

>>> wn.synsets('car')
[Synset('car.n.01'), Synset('car.n.02'), Synset('car.n.03'), Synset('car.n.04'), Synset('cable_car.n.01')]
>>> wn.synsets('auto')
[Synset('car.n.01')]
>>> wn.synsets('friend')
[Synset('friend.n.01'), Synset('ally.n.02'), Synset('acquaintance.n.03'), Synset('supporter.n.01'), Synset('friend.n.05')]

And when you look into the different senses that each lemma_name contains, you get an recursive chain of lemmas of synsets of lemmas of synsets ...:
