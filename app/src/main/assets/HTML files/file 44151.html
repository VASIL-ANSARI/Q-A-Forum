Use scipy.spatial.distance (http://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/spatial.distance.html), and in particular, cdist().

>>> x = [1,2,3,4,5]
>>> y = [4,1,7,8,1]
>>> xy = zip(x, y)
>>> xy
[(1, 4), (2, 1), (3, 7), (4, 8), (5, 1)]
>>> import scipy.spatial.distance as ssd
>>> ssd.cdist(xy, xy)
array([[ 0.        ,  3.16227766,  3.60555128,  5.        ,  5.        ],
       [ 3.16227766,  0.        ,  6.08276253,  7.28010989,  3.        ],
       [ 3.60555128,  6.08276253,  0.        ,  1.41421356,  6.32455532],
       [ 5.        ,  7.28010989,  1.41421356,  0.        ,  7.07106781],
       [ 5.        ,  3.        ,  6.32455532,  7.07106781,  0.        ]])
