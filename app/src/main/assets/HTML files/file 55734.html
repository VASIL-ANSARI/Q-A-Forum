I searched this topic since 2-3 month ago and found OkHttp 's Authenticator. You can use it . There is one link here : refreshing-oauth-token-using-retrofit-without-modifying-all-calls

It working like : If your request returns 401 , then Authenticator moves in , and refresh your token. But dont forget to return null or put any try limit .If you dont limit,it will try to refresh multiple times when your refresh fails and also use basic HttpUrlConnection instead of Retrofit when you are refreshing your token. Also i had a question in here , maybe you will take a look at : android-retrofit2-refresh-oauth-2-token

Also for example if you have a token and you need to refresh it per 3 hours . You can write an Interceptor too. In Interceptor : compare time and refresh your token without getting any 401 response.
