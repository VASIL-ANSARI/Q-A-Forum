You need to loop through the list returned by find_all():

for i in soup.find_all('tag'):
    txt = i.text
    if 'string' not in txt:
        print txt

Alternatively, you could create another list where the unwanted tags are filtered out:

filtered_lst = [i for i in soup.find_all('tag') if 'string' not in i.text]

This will give you a list of Tag objects similar to that returned by find_all(), but with only the tags that do not include 'string' in their .text. After that you can do what you want it it:

for tag in filtered_lst:
    print tag.text

Update:

On the other hand, if your goal is to simply filter 'string' from the text of the tag, you can use the replace() method of strings:

for tag in soup.find_all('tag'):
    print tag.text.replace('string', '')

However, keep in mind that this will remove 'string' wherever it is in the text, and may leave unwanted whitespace to be removed later. For example:

>>> 'hellostring string'.replace('string', '')
'hello '
