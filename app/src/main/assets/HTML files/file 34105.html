I am not sure what you're trying to do but in your sample project above, PropertiesUtil extends from PropertyPlaceholderConfigurer but your XML configuration refers to PropertyPlaceholderConfigurer and not your sub-class. So if that's all you have, your class is not even invoked.

I tried that locally with the following config and it worked:

<bean id="applicationProperties" class="com.foo.yourpackage.PropertiesUtil">
    <property name="location" value="classpath:app.properties"/>
</bean>

Now, I understand you're trying to access to the properties in your code.The Environment abstraction has all that you need, check this blog post and the javadoc of Environment for more details.
