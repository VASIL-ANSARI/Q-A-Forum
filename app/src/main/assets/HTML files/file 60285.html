The error message show the root of error:

 Window powershell updated your execution policy suceffully, but the 
 setting is overridden by a policy defined at a more specific scope.
 Due to the override, your shell will retain its current effective
 execution policy of remoteassigned.

So, there is a group policy setting for the current user assigned to remoteassigned and can't be changed by the command.

Modify the setting in the group policy as in the given steps:

 run: gpedit.msc

In the left tree, select:

user configuration -> administrative templates-> windows components -> windows powershell

turn on script execution -> enabled As in the figure: If your machine is a member of domain and you log as a domain user, there may be a restriction for modification.

In that case contact the Domain administrator to modify your group policy.
