This is a very common problem, and it's an encoding issue. You will need to encode your video using H264 as the video codec, and AAC as the audio codec.

When you use H264 as your video codec, you will also need to choose a profile. Higher profiles require more CPU power to decode and are able to generate better looking videos at same bitrate. You should always choose the best profile your target devices support.

For example, the max supported profile for Desktop browsers, iPhone 4S+, iPad 2+, Android 4.x+ tablets, Xbox 360, Playstation 3 is High profile.

Most devices also have a maximum resolution and bitrate they can handle. That is usually expressed in as a H.264 level.

You can re-encode your video using the ffmpeg utility.

I personally use a website called CloudConvert, you just have to set the conversion options to:

  • Video Codec: H264
  • Audio Codec: AAC
  • Leave the other options at their default values
