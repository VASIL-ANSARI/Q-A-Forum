First things first, man, you aren't terminating your HTML tags:

 <div id="slider">
 <div class="slide">
     <a href="link"><img src="imglink">
 <div class="slide-block">
 <h4>Text</h4>
 <p>Subtext</p>

Do you see what is wrong with this? The browser can't figure out the children of .slider, because I can't figure out the children of .slider.

How to fix this, in case you don't know

Divs go like this:

<div class='slider'><!-- content inside --></div>

Sooo... what's the problem?

Improperly formatting HTML is good at hiding flaws in the HTML structure. Properly formatted, take a look at your code:

<div id="wrapper">
  <div id="slider">
    <div class="slide">
      <a href="link"><img src="imglink">
        <div class="slide-block">
          <h4>Text</h4>
          <p>Subtext</p>
        </div>
      </a>
    </div>
    <div id="slider">
      <div class="slide">
        <a href="link"><img src="imglink">
          <div class="slide-block">
            <h4>Text</h4>
            <p>Subtext</p>
          </div>
        </a>
      </div>
      <div id="slider">
        <div class="slide">
          <a href="link"><img src="imglink">
            <div class="slide-block">
              <h4>Text</h4>
              <p>Subtext</p>
            </div>
          </a>
        </div>

Do you see how the wrapper and the first pieces were never properly terminated? The browser is likely very confused with how to interpret your HTML.
